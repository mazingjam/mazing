# Research Notes: GrowUp First 90 Days

Updated: 2026-06-16

## What This Research Supports

This document supports the first three actions in the 90-day plan:

1. Create a 12-month product map with rough COGS and margin.
2. Build a landing page with a "build first box" flow.
3. Create a prototype of the month 1 book.

## Market And Positioning

GrowUp should be positioned as a personalized baby development subscription, not a generic baby box. The strongest wedge is the combination of:

- Month-by-month physical products.
- Personalization from the child's profile.
- A companion app that collects preferences and unlocks timely guidance.
- Giftability for grandparents, friends and baby showers.

The first box must create emotional proof. A parent should feel: "this was made for my baby", not "this is a bundle of baby products".

## Buyer Segments

### Primary

Expecting parents and parents with babies aged 0-3 months.

Core trigger:

- Preparing for birth.
- Seeking routines.
- Wanting meaningful keepsakes.
- Feeling overwhelmed by what to buy next.

### Secondary

Gift buyers: grandparents, friends, colleagues and baby shower groups.

Core trigger:

- Wanting a thoughtful gift that continues after birth.
- Wanting something more personal than clothes or flowers.

## Subscription Implications

The product should avoid a "random monthly surprise" frame. Parents of babies need trust and relevance. The box should feel curated, predictable enough to trust and personalized enough to delight.

Recommended subscription architecture:

- Essentials: one timed physical product each month.
- Personal: physical product plus personalization and app-linked selections.
- Gift: prepaid 3, 6 or 12 months.

## Safety And Compliance Considerations

Product development should treat safety as part of the product map, not as a late-stage checklist.

Key implications for 0-36 months:

- Avoid small parts and detachable components.
- Track materials, batch, supplier, test reports and care instructions.
- Use age grading per product and per monthly box.
- Avoid marketing visuals that show unsafe sleep conditions for newborns.
- Keep product pages and inserts careful: no medical claims, no sleep guarantees.

- EU toy safety overview: https://single-market-economy.ec.europa.eu/sectors/toys/toy-safety_en

## Landing Page Implications

The website should sell the first emotional moment before asking for a subscription.

The highest-intent flow:

1. Enter child's name.
2. Pick birth month.
3. Pick avatar basics.
4. See a personalized first-book preview.
5. Choose subscription or gift.

The preview is more important than a long marketing page. It turns abstract personalization into something visible.

## Product Cost Assumptions

These numbers are planning assumptions for early modeling, not supplier quotes.

- Subscription price target: 299 SEK per month for Personal.
- Gross margin target before paid media: 55-65%.
- Fulfillment and packaging must be modeled separately from product COGS.
- Personalized printed products have higher setup complexity but stronger retention and ad creative value.

## Open Research Needed Before Supplier Outreach

- Board book print MOQ and unit cost at 500, 1,000 and 5,000 units.
- Variable-print workflow cost for personalized covers/interiors.
- Textile certification requirements for snutte and baby clothing.
- Toy testing lab quote for 0-36 month products.
- Shipping cost by package size and weight in Sweden, EU and potential Nordics expansion.
