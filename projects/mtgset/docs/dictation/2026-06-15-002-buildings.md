# Dictation 2026-06-15 002: Buildings

## Raw Notes

- "RAM" means Ravnica. The prior dictation/transcription was wrong there.
- Buildings are a card type that is somewhat a mix between planeswalkers and creatures.
- Buildings have power and toughness.
- Their toughness is persistent and may be called durability.
- Buildings can be attacked directly.
- Buildings cannot attack.
- Buildings can block.
- Buildings deal combat damage.
- Durability is closer to persistent damage than loyalty.
- Buildings are often also artifacts or enchantments, but that is not required.
- Buildings can block any attacking creature as though they were creatures.
- Buildings can be targeted by wording that targets any target, a permanent, or a Building.

## Immediate Interpretation

- Buildings are permanents with combat relevance.
- They may deal damage using power, but damage marked on them persists across turns via durability.
- They are attackable objects like planeswalkers/battles, but use creature-like stats.
- `durability` is the current working name for persistent toughness, but not final.
- Buildings are defensive permanents: they cannot attack, but they can block and deal combat damage.
- Building type lines can include artifact or enchantment, but `Building` does not require either.
- For blocking, Buildings function like creatures and can block any attacking creature.
- Targeting hooks: `any target`, `target permanent`, and `target Building` can target Buildings.

## Open Rules Questions

- Can noncombat damage target Buildings?
- Are Buildings affected by creature removal, permanent removal, or their own removal category?
- Are Buildings affected by creature-specific removal, or only permanent/building-specific removal?
