# Dictation 2026-06-15 004: Gold Costs

## Raw Notes

- Gold counters themselves are simply counters.
- Many cards can refer to gold counters.
- A new resource exists as a gold coin added to card or ability costs.
- Example: a creature card may cost `{1}{B}` plus one gold.
- A gold cost is paid by removing a gold counter from a permanent you control.

## Immediate Interpretation

- `Gold counter` is a normal counter with no intrinsic rules meaning.
- `Gold coin` is a cost symbol or resource requirement.
- Paying one gold means removing one gold counter from a permanent you control.
- This can appear in mana costs and activated ability costs.

## Open Rules Questions

- What exact symbol represents gold in costs?
- Can a spell with a gold cost be cast if you control no permanents with gold counters?
- Can one permanent pay multiple gold costs by removing multiple counters?
- Can gold costs be affected by cost-modification effects?
- Does removing a gold counter as a cost target the permanent? Current assumption: no.
