# Factions

Short lore and concept-art direction for the current draft structure.

## WBU: Old Money + Government

Image: `../images/factions/wbu-old-money-government.png`

The old houses do not rule because they are loved. They rule because every charter, court, bank, registry, and inheritance office has their seal somewhere in the foundation. Gold is legitimacy here: counted, witnessed, taxed, inherited, and weaponized through paperwork.

Image prompt: Marble ministry-bank interior, aristocratic bureaucrats, glowing gold counters embedded in ledgers and seals, enchantment-like architecture, restrained opulence, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## RWU: Individualism + Military + Progress

Image: `../images/factions/rwu-progress-military.png`

This faction sells the future as a parade. Heroes are minted, uniforms are polished, patents become weapons, and public works become monuments to ambition. Gold counters are medals, funding, upgrades, and proof that the system has chosen you.

Image prompt: Bright militarized civic plaza with banners, prototype airships, decorated soldiers and inventors, gold medals and upgrade tokens, optimistic but imperial mood, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## RBU: Bribe

Image: `../images/factions/rbu-bribe.png`

Nothing here is illegal if the right person profits. The faction lives in private rooms, rigged hearings, black-market docks, encrypted ledgers, and smiles that cost more than swords. Bribe is not corruption to them; it is the real law underneath the written one.

Image prompt: Shadowy canal market and velvet backroom, rogues, advisors, coin-lit contracts, blue-black-red neon alchemy, bribery and intrigue, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## WBR: Human Citizens

Image: `../images/factions/wbr-human-citizens.png`

The crowd is a resource, a mandate, and a threat. Citizens gather in plazas, debtor halls, barracks, and torchlit assemblies, pulled between solidarity and exploitation. Gold marks wages, debt, patronage, and bought loyalty.

Image prompt: Torchlit civic plaza packed with human citizens, debt banners, public speakers, soldiers, coin marks, volatile mass politics, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## GR: Collapse

Image: `../images/factions/gr-collapse.png`

The city does not fall all at once. It cracks, blooms, burns, and then remembers it was never separate from the wild. GR is nature as riot and rupture: roads split by roots, Buildings broken open, gold swallowed by vines and fire.

Image prompt: Overgrown city street collapsing under roots and wildfire, broken buildings, beasts and shamans, gold coins sinking into moss, red-green catastrophe, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## GU: Reclamation

Image: `../images/factions/gu-reclamation.png`

GU does not destroy civilization so much as reclassify it. Archives become reefs, laboratories become wetlands, and forgotten machines learn to photosynthesize. Its magic is adaptation, memory, counter-movement, and the intelligence of living systems.

Image prompt: Flooded archive greenhouse, roots through bookshelves and machines, bioluminescent water, druids and merfolk researchers, blue-green reclamation, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## Mono-G: Force of Nature

Image: `../images/factions/mono-g-force-of-nature.png`

Mono-green is not a faction. It is the bill coming due. It does not negotiate with governments, armies, syndicates, or crowds. It grows through vaults, sleeps under roads, and wakes when the gold has become too heavy for the world to bear.

Image prompt: Primeval forest force breaking through a gilded city, enormous roots, beasts, moss-covered vaults, natural judgment, green elemental power, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## RGU: Nature Convergence

Image: `../images/factions/rgu-nature-convergence.png`

Where red storm, blue adaptation, and green inevitability meet, nature becomes a system with teeth. RGU is weather, mutation, ecology, and catastrophe acting as one intelligence. It does not reject progress; it outgrows it.

Image prompt: Elemental storm over a rewilded city, lightning mycelium, adaptive creatures, flooded streets, red-blue-green ecological intelligence, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## WB: Humanity

Image: `../images/factions/wb-humanity.png`

WB is the human cost. It counts names, buries bodies, issues ration papers, keeps shrines lit, and asks what survives when institutions and nature both demand payment. It is mortality, community, grief, obligation, and endurance.

Image prompt: Dim tenement shrine and public registry, human citizens grieving and helping each other, candles, ledgers, white-black humanity theme, solemn fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## GW: Unity of Humanity and Nature

Image: `../images/factions/gw-unity.png`

GW believes the split can still be healed. Its envoys plant gardens in courtyards, broker treaties with the wild, and build sanctuaries without walls. It is not naive peace; it is hard, daily repair.

Image prompt: Human citizens and nature spirits building a shared garden sanctuary, open walls, saplings, white-green accord, hopeful but grounded fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.

## GB: Doom

Image: `../images/factions/gb-doom.png`

GB follows the rot all the way down. It knows every empire composts eventually, every contract ends in soil, and every buried thing becomes leverage. Doom is not tragedy here; it is process.

Image prompt: Dark compost cathedral of roots and bones, black-green decay, grave orchard, gold counters tarnished in soil, ominous cycle of death and growth, fantasy trading-card concept art, no text.
