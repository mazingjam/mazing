# Energy System

Sunder uses multiple energy colors instead of one generic energy pool.

## Colors

### Red

Red is the color of brute force and blunt action.

Typical red effects:

- Deal direct damage.
- Attack multiple times.
- Break armor or guards.
- Push through penalties.
- Take risky actions for immediate output.

Red can still support, draw, or manipulate when needed, but it should usually do so through force, sacrifice, or momentum.

### Green

Green is the color of support and noncombatant-oriented play.

Typical green effects:

- Heal or prevent damage.
- Protect allies.
- Summon or coordinate companions.
- Move, rescue, escort, or reposition.
- Improve future turns through growth or preparation.

Green can still deal damage, but it should often do so through allies, beasts, terrain, or setup.

### Blue

Blue is the color of cunning and spells.

Typical blue effects:

- Draw, discard, search, or reorder cards.
- Copy or modify effects.
- Dodge, counter, delay, or redirect.
- Deal spell damage.
- Manipulate enemy intent or encounter rules.

Blue can still hit hard, but it should often require timing, setup, or conversion.

### White

White is flexible energy. It can pay for any color.

Design use:

- Smooth awkward hands.
- Make heroes less brittle.
- Act as rare premium energy.
- Support hybrid decks.

White should be valuable because it reduces color pressure.

### Black

Black is restricted energy. It can only pay black costs.

Design use:

- Corruption, curses, demons, forbidden power, sacrifice, death, decay, or void effects.
- High-impact cards that cannot be casually paid with flexible energy.
- A separate economy for dangerous power.

Black should feel tempting and constrained.

## Hero Energy Bar

Each hero card provides a base energy bar: a fixed amount and color mix available each turn unless modified.

Examples:

- Savage: `Red Red White`
- Oracle: `Blue Blue White`
- Noble: `Green White White`
- Elementalist: `Red Green Blue`
- Demon-bound hero: `Black Red White`

These are examples only, not locked designs.

## Energy Added By Cards

Cards can increase available energy.

Possible models:

- Immediate: gain energy this turn.
- Persistent: increase the hero energy bar for the rest of the encounter.
- Charged: place energy tokens that can be spent later.
- Conditional: gain energy when a trigger happens.
- Exhaustible: a card or item can be tapped/used once per encounter.

## Design Risks

- Too many colors can cause hand frustration.
- White can flatten identity if too common.
- Black needs a clear reason to exist beyond being a fourth color.
- Energy-generating cards can create runaway turns if they are too efficient.
