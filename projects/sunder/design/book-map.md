# Book Map

The Sunder map is a physical book. Opening the book creates the board.

## Core Idea

Each spread represents a section of the run. The left and right pages together show paths, encounters, opportunities, hazards, and page-specific rules.

Players generally move from left to right through a spread, but some spreads may:

- Branch into multiple paths.
- Allow players to split up.
- Offer optional side encounters.
- Lock routes based on earlier choices.
- Contain hidden or delayed events.
- Change when the page is revisited or flipped.

## Why A Book

The book should solve the flat-map problem by making the route tactile and surprising. Turning a page should feel like entering a new chapter, not just revealing another node graph.

The book also allows:

- Strong visual identity for each area.
- Page-specific rules printed directly on the board.
- Physical route constraints.
- Encounter variety without many loose boards.
- Narrative pacing without requiring a full campaign book.

## Spread Anatomy

A spread may contain:

- Start edge.
- Exit edge.
- Encounter nodes.
- Opportunity nodes.
- Branching paths.
- Shared party spaces.
- Split-party lanes.
- Page rule boxes.
- Reward icons.
- Threat clocks.
- Boss or elite markers.

## Split Party Principles

Splitting the party should be a meaningful choice, not a punishment by default.

Good reasons to split:

- Cover more rewards.
- Stop a threat clock.
- Rescue an NPC.
- Scout a safer route.
- Activate two mechanisms at once.

Costs of splitting:

- Less shared defense.
- Limited card assistance.
- Harder enemy focus control.
- Separate encounter timers.
- Risk of being trapped or delayed.

## Initial Prototype Target

Start with a small paper prototype:

- 3 spreads.
- 2-4 heroes.
- One branch per spread.
- One split-party opportunity.
- One boss spread.
